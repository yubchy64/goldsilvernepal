# Design System

## Overview
Firecrawl’s visual identity is clean, bright, and highly product-focused, with a technical startup feel softened by warm orange accents. The interface uses lots of white space, subtle borders, and gentle shadows to create a light, airy canvas that keeps attention on the core value proposition and interactive demos. Overall, it communicates speed, clarity, and developer trust with a polished but understated modern aesthetic.

## Colors
- **Primary/Accent** (**#fa5d19**): The signature orange used for key CTAs, highlighted text, active states, and brand moments. It is energetic and highly visible without feeling neon.
- **Background** (**#f9f9f9**): The dominant page and card background, creating a very light off-white surface that reduces contrast harshness.
- **Text** (**#262626**): Main body and headline text color, a near-black charcoal that feels softer than pure black while maintaining strong readability.
- **Border/Divider** (**#e5e7eb**): Thin neutral gray used for card outlines, inputs, separators, and subtle UI framing.
- **White** (**#ffffff**): Used for primary text on orange buttons and as the base for elevated panels and control surfaces.
- **Muted Gray** (**#9ca3af** / similar UI gray): Seen in secondary navigation, icons, and placeholder elements to reduce emphasis.
- **Soft Shadow Tint** (**rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.02–0.05)**): Used in large card and floating panel shadows for depth without visual heaviness.

## Typography
- **Headlines**: `Suisse` (with system sans fallbacks), medium weight. H1s are large and compact, around 44px/48px with negative letter spacing for a crisp, premium look. H2s are 36px/40px and similarly tight; smaller headings stay in the 18px–22px range with slightly lighter emphasis.
- **Body**: `Suisse`, regular weight 400. Standard body copy is 16px/26px with subtle negative letter spacing, giving the text a refined, modern editorial feel.
- **Labels/Captions**: `Suisse`, medium-ish weight around 450, usually 16px or smaller. Labels are simple and understated, used for navigation, pills, button text, and helper UI.

## Components
- **Buttons**: Rounded rectangular buttons with a 10px radius and compact padding. Primary buttons are filled with bright orange (#fa5d19), white text, and a soft layered shadow/inset highlight that makes them feel tactile and prominent. Secondary buttons are mostly text-like or transparent, with dark text and minimal visual treatment.
- **Cards**: Very light gray or white surfaces with 1px neutral borders, 8px corner radius, and almost no visible shadow. Larger floating panels use extremely soft, multi-layer shadows to create depth while staying subtle and airy.
- **Inputs**: Input surfaces are white with thin gray borders and generous rounding. They appear understated and clean, with focus states implied by elevation and contrast rather than heavy outlines.
- **Pills/Tabs**: Small rounded chips with soft gray backgrounds and clear active-state differentiation. Active chips use stronger contrast and the orange accent to indicate selection.
- **Navigation**: Top navigation is minimal, text-first, and evenly spaced. Links are dark gray/charcoal, with tiny dropdown indicators and a reserved, professional feel.

## Spacing & Elevation
The spacing system feels modular and restrained, with common steps around 2px, 10px, 20px, 28px, and 40px. Layouts rely on generous whitespace and clear vertical separation rather than dense grouping. Elevation is subtle: most elements are flat, while floating panels and hero demo blocks use soft, diffuse shadows to suggest lift without overpowering the page.

## Do's and Don'ts
- Do: Use bright orange sparingly for primary actions and key emphasis.
- Don't: Overuse accent color across large surfaces or secondary controls.
- Do: Keep surfaces light, neutral, and low-contrast with subtle borders.
- Don't: Introduce heavy shadows, dark panels, or strong visual noise.
- Do: Use large, tight headline typography with a modern sans-serif feel.
- Don't: Mix in decorative, serif, or overly playful fonts.
- Do: Favor compact rounded corners and soft, refined component shapes.
- Don't: Use sharp 0-radius geometry or overly bubbly pill styling everywhere.
- Do: Preserve lots of whitespace and a calm, premium SaaS layout.
- Don't: Pack content too tightly or create dense, dashboard-like clutter.
